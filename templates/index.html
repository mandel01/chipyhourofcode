FAQ

What is Hour of Code?

The Hour of Code is a one-hour introduction to computer science, designed to demystify code and show that anybody can learn the basics. It is held as part of Computer Science Education Week, and we hope it inspires a lifetime of fun with computers!

Why computer science?

Every student should have the opportunity to learn computer science. It helps nurture problem-solving skills, logic and creativity. By starting early, students will have a foundation for success in any 21st-century career path. See more stats on code.org.

Do I need a laptop?

If you want to use a laptop and mouse, bring them. We will also have activities for smartphones or tablets, and one that requires no computer at all.

Should I bring an adult?

Yes, please! The target audience for our event are students new to programming, ages 5 - 10. We would prefer if your adult stays to make sure you're having fun. (We'll have coffee and snacks for them.)

But, I don't know how to code...

Great! We want to introduce you to programming, and to some Chicago programmers.
